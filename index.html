questions to work in naturally
01 — most important
"Is this something you're doing through Night officially, or more your Noonchimes project?"
You already know the answer is probably both — but his answer tells you what he's comfortable claiming. Honest answer = trustworthy. Overclaim = slow down.
02
"What does the split look like on brand deals you bring in?"
Standard is 15–20%. Anything above 25% is unusual. Asking this is completely normal and professional — it won't scare him off.
03
"The 4th Wall thing — I'd love to see what the terms look like before anything's signed."
He acted before you agreed to anything. Seeing paperwork is always your right. Frame it as being thorough, not distrustful.
04
"When you mention Hot Topic — is that a paid partnership or the affiliate program?"
This immediately shows whether he knows the difference. If he knows, good sign. If he doesn't, recalibrate expectations on that specific promise.
05
"What does the first 90 days actually look like practically?"
Grounds the hype in reality. A good answer is specific — deals, timelines, who you're talking to. A bad answer is vague vibes.
listen for these
Good: He's specific about timelines, names actual contacts at brands, mentions other creators at your scale he's currently working with.
Good: He's honest that he's a coordinator, not a senior manager — and frames it as "I'm building this out" rather than overclaiming authority.
Good: He asks about your content, your goals, what you want — not just what he can sell. A manager who only pitches isn't really managing.
Watch for: Pressure to sign or agree to something tonight. Legitimate deals have paperwork that takes days. Nothing real requires a same-night commitment.
Watch for: Vagueness on the 4th Wall terms or resistance to showing you the contract. You have every right to read anything before it represents you.
Watch for: He can only name Johnnie Guilbert when asked about his roster. One name at a completely different scale isn't a track record with smaller creators.
